Generation 9 Raid Guide

Six Star Pokémon

Amoongus (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Energy Ball:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
has a 10% chance of lowering the target's Special Defense by one stage
Foul Play:Category: Physical / Power: 95 / Accuracy: 100%
Uses the target's Attack Stat to calculate damage
Spore:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Puts the target to sleep
Sludge Bomb:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 30% chance of poisoning the target
Grassy Terrain:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - Boosts the power of Grass-type moves used by affected Pokémon by 30%
  • - At the end of each turn, restores the HP of each affected Pokémon by 1/16 of its maximum HP
  • - The power of Bulldoze, Earthquake, and Magnitude is halved against affected targets
Abilities:
Effect Spore:Category: Status
When coming into contact with this Pokemon, there is a 30% change of poison, paralysis, or sleep
Regenerator:Category: Status
Does not effect Raid
Tips:
    The biggest hurddle you're going to face against Amoongus is its combination Spore and Grassy Terain:
  • - Grassy Terain will boost its grass powered moves and negate ability moves like Hadron Engine and Electric Surge.
  • - Spore has a 20% chance of causing a status effect that skips your turn unless you Cheer Heal.
Pokemon with Electric Terrain are a huge asset here. Not only will it negate the grassy terrain, but it will keep you and your teamates from suffering sleep effects from spore. If you don't have the luxury of Electric Terrain, moves like Taunt and Safeguard, and held items like Safety Goggles are good alternatives.
Otherwise Amoongus has better Base Special Deffense meaning bringing a physical attacker that takes its Tera type into account is the best offense. Keep in mind, if your physical attacker is weak to Dark moves, Foul Play will hit harder the higher your attack stat and do super effective damage on top. Additonally, unless you have a held item like Protective Pads or Punching Gloves doing a physical attack can activate its Effect Spore ability if your encounter has it.

Annihilape (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Close Combat:Category: Physical / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the users Defense stat and Special Defense stat by one stage each
Shadow Claw:Category: Physical / Power: 70 / Accuracy: 100%
Has an increased critical hit ratio
Assurance:Category: Physical / Power: 60 / Accuracy: 100%
Will deal double damage if the target has already received damage on the turn it is used; including self-inflicted and indirect damage
Focus Energy:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Increases the user's critical hit ratio by two stages
Bulk Up:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the user's Attack stat and Defense stat by one stage each
Rage Fist:Category: Physical / Power: 50 / Accuracy: 100%
Its power increases by 50 each time the user was hit during a battle, by a move that causes direct damage, up to a maximum of 350
Abilities:
Vital Spirit:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from falling asleep
Inner Focus:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from flinching
Defiant:Category: Status
Boosts the Pokémon's Attack stat sharply when its stats are lowered
Tips:
    The biggest hurddle you're going to face against Annihilape is its ability to passively and actively increase its Attack stat.
  • - Moves like Bulk Up, Rage Fist and its hidden ability Defiant will all contribute to a powerhouse attack that can one hit most Pokémon.
  • - Additionally, Focus Energy will boost its ability to perform cricital hits which will bypass moves like reflect.
Pokemon with Taunt are a huge asset here. It will negate Annihilapes ability to use status moves like Bulk Up or Focus Energy. Additionally, do your best to avoid moves that have secondary abilities that increase or decrease the opponents stats, as Defiant will increase Annihilapes Attack power.
Annihilape has better Base Special Deffense meaning bringing a Physical Attacker seems like a better play, however the difference between its Special Defense and Defense are minimal and bringing a Physical Attacker runs the risk of boosting the damage Rage Fist does on every hit. A Special Attacker, taking into account Tera type, is the best offense here. As for durability, note that all of Annihilapes attacks are physical, so bringing a Pokémon with high defense is key.

Armarogue (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Armor Canon:Category: Special / Power: 120 / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the users Defense stat and Special Defense stat by one stage each
Psychic:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a 10% chance of lowering the targets Special Defense stat by one stage
Night Shade:Category: Special / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Inflicts damage equal to the users level
Will-O-Wisp:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 85%
Inflicts a burn on the target
Calm Mind:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Raises the user's Special Attack and Special Defense stats by one stage each
Sunny Day:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - The base power of Fire moves will be increased by 50%
  • - The base power of Water moves will be decreased by 50%
Abilities:
Flash Fire:Category: Status
Makes the Pokémon immune to Fire-type moves and will activate when hit by one. When activated, the power of the Pokémons Fire moves is increased by 50%
Weak Armor:Category: Status
When hit by a physical move, its Defense is decreased by one stage and its Speed is increased by two stages
Tips:
The biggest hurddle you're going to face against Armarogue are its moves Sunny Day and Calm Mind.
Pokemon with Rain Dance are a huge asset here. It will override Armarogues Sunny Day and decrease the power of its base fire moves by 50% as well as increase the base power of water moves by 50%. Make sure to bring a Pokemon with water type moves here unless its Tera type makes water not an option.
Pokemon with Taunt are also great here to prevent status moves like Sunny Day, Calm Mind, and Will-O-Wisp.
Armarogue has better Base Deffense meaning bringing a Special Attacker, taking into account Tera type, is a safer choice here. Note that special attacks will also prevent Armarogues Weak Armor from activating increasing its speed. However an argument could be made that bringing a Physical attacker against an Armarogue with Weak Armor is actually more helpful because it will lower its defense stat with each hit making it more susceptible to further physical attacks, but only if it has Weak Armor.

Baxcalibur (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Icicle Spear:Category: Physical / Power: 25 / Accuracy: 100%
Has a chance of hitting the target 2-5 times per use
Dragon Rush:Category: Physical / Power: 100 / Accuracy: 75%
Has a 20% chance of causing the target to flinch
Body Press:Category: Physical / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Uses the users Defense stat to calculate damage
Snowscape:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
  • - All Ice-type Pokémon have their Defense raised by 50% for the duration of the snowstorm
  • - Pokémon with the Ice Body Ability recover 1/16th of their maximum HP each round
Abilities:
Thermal Exchange:Category: Status
  • - Boosts the Attack stat when the Pokémon is hit by a Fire-type move
  • - Cannot be burned
Ice Body:Category: Status
Gradually regains 1/16th HP in snow
Tips:
    The biggest hurddle you're going to face against Baxcalibur are its Abilities Thermal Exchange and Ice Body.
  • - Thermal Exchange will make it stronger physically against a Fire Attacker and prevent burns.
  • - Ice Body will heal this pokemon when it uses its move Snowscape.
Consider also bringing a pokemon that knows Taunt, Sunny Day, or Rain Dance as that will prevent or remove any snow effect, keeping Baxcalibur from raising its defense passively. Keep in mind that certain weather effects can help or hinder your teammates abilities and moves.
Baxcalibur has an insanely high Physical attack, so you're going to want to bring a Pokémon with High Defense. The base Special Defense and Physical Defense are close, so bring a Special Attacker if you cant rely on you or your teammates preventing Snowscape. Absolutely Do Not bring a fire type attacker regardless of Tera Type.

Blissey (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Dazzling Gleam:Category: Special / Power: 80 / Accuracy: 100%
Secondary ability does not effect Raid
Defense Curl:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Increases the user's Defense by 1 stage
Sing:Category: Physical / Power: - / Accuracy: 55%
Puts the target to sleep
Light Screen:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: - %
Reduces damage from special attacks by about 1/3rd
Hyper Voice:Category: Special / Power: 90 / Accuracy: 100%
  • - Can hit Pokémon behind a substitute
  • - Damage dealt will be halved when used against a Pokémon with the Punk Rock Ability
Abilities:
Natural Cure:Category: Status
Does not effect Raid
Serene Grace:Category: Status
Doubles the chance of a move (with additional status effects) having those additional status effects
Healer:Category: Status
Does not effect Raid
Tips:
    The biggest hurddle you're going to face against Blissey is its incredibly high Health and Special Defense as well as its status moves.
  • - Blissey has one of the highest Health Pools of any Pokémon.
  • - Additionally, status moves like Sing, Defense Curl, and Light Screen will make it tanky against most attackers.
Pokemon with Taunt are a huge asset here. It will negate Blisseys status abilities keeping its physical defense extremely low, and preventing your Pokémon from being put to sleep- requiring a heal cheer to wake them. Moves and abilities like Electric Terrain, Electric Surge, Hadron Engine, and Safeguard are also effective, however none of them will prevent defense curl or light screen.
Because Blissey balances its high health with an extremely low defense, Physical Attackers here are key even if you don't have a Pokémon that knows taunt. Light Screen as well as having a decently high base Special Defense will reduce incoming damage from special attacks, making them almost worthless in this Raid.

Bombirdier (lvl. 90)
Strong Against:
Moves:
Rock Slide:Category: Physical / Power: 75 / Accuracy: 90%
Has a 30% chance of causing the target to flinch
Acrobatics:Category: Physical / Power: 55 / Accuracy: 100%
Its power is doubled to 110 if the user has no held item
Knock Off:Category: Physical / Power: 65 / Accuracy: 100%
Base power will be boosted by 50% if the target has a held item
Feather Dance:Category: Status / Power: - / Accuracy: 100%
Lowers the target's Attack stat by two stages
Abilities:
Big Pecks:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from having its Defense stat lowered
Keen Eye:Category: Status
Prevents the Pokémon from having its accuracy from being lowered
Rocky Payload:Category: Status
Will increase Damage, dealt by the user, from Rock moves by 50%
Tips:
    Bombirdier relies on Rocky Payload, and its secondary abilities for each of its moves to do massive damage.
  • - If you're holding an item, Knock Off will do 97.5 base damage
  • - If you're not holding an item, Acrobatics will do 110 base damage
  • - If Bombirdier has Rocky Payload, Rock Slide will do 112.5 base damage
The best Pokémon to bring to this Raid are Rock Types that are not holding an item. The most damage Bombirdier can do to these Pokémon is 110 base, and because Rock Slide and Acrobatics are less effective against Rock types you negate the additional damage from secondary effects. Additionally, not holding an item will negate the extra damage done from Knock Off.
Because Feather Dance lowers physical attacks, ensure that you also have the ability to use Taunt if your primary attacks rely on Physical rather than Special.
Because Bombirdier has equal Defense and Special Defense stats it does not matter what kind of attacker you bring. The exception is if you do not have Taunt to negate Feather Dance, in which case a Rock Type Special Attacker is best.
